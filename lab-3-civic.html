AT-LAB III · Civic · Participation · Collective AI Literacy

AT-LAB III —
Civic

Two linked civic interventions exploring collective AI literacy through participatory design, LARP, and public-space imagination — Placemaking Week Europe 2025, Reggio Emilia

Placemaking Week Europe 2025 Reggio Emilia, Italy Promptare / PublicTwin Civic AI Literacy Semiotic Protocol

Context — Two Probes, One AT-LAB

AT-LAB III marked the civic turn in the trajectory. Where AT-LAB I engaged citizens through embodiment in a health context, and AT-LAB II engaged artists through curation in a classroom context, AT-LAB III brought the AI literacy question into public space — to citizens imagining their future city, to collective speculation about what AI does to how we inhabit shared environments.

AT-LAB III consisted of two linked interventions — a macro-probe and a micro-probe — both conducted in the context of Placemaking Week Europe 2025 in Reggio Emilia, Italy. Together they form a double structure: one testing how collective imagination works at scale; the other testing how interpretive ambiguity functions in a small-group cognitive game.

The Two Interventions

MACRO PROBE

Promptare / PublicTwin

Created for Placemaking Week Europe 2025 in Reggio Emilia, in collaboration with PublicTwin (Michał Kasperski & Jacek Baczkowski). A 75-minute co-creation workshop format using prompt cards, QR-code input, mobile participation interfaces, and live voting around AI-generated images of public-space scenarios. Participants (citizens, urban planners, designers) co-created prompts and collectively evaluated AI image outputs, exploring how collective imagination could shape future urban environments. The intervention asked: when a community uses AI to imagine its own city, who controls the framing? Whose imagination is amplified, and whose is erased?

MICRO PROBE

Semiotic Protocol

A small-group interpretive LARP (Live Action Role Play) activity in which participants matched 24 AI-generated images to 8 speculative prompts. The activity was designed to trigger productive ambiguity: images were deliberately polysemous, prompts were deliberately open. The goal was not consensus but productive disagreement — to reveal how people make sense of AI-generated imagery through projection, negotiation, and contestation. Documentation logged rationale types, interpretation strategies, and the emergence of collective meaning through debate.

Research Evidence

Participatory Documentation
  • Workshop feedback forms from Promptare session
  • Observation notes from live voting sequences
  • Documentation of participant-generated prompt texts
  • Voting pattern data from mobile participation interface
  • Micro-probe: image-prompt matching responses from all participants, coded by rationale type (metaphorical, literal, associative, critical)
Key Findings
  • Civic groups engage AI effectively through play and speculation
  • Literacy here is less individual mastery than collective reflection and negotiated meaning-making
  • The macro/micro probe combination revealed different registers of AI engagement: public imagination vs. interpretive close reading
  • Participants used civic and spatial language to critique AI bias spontaneously — confirming that context activates literacy
  • Collective disagreement proved epistemically more productive than consensus

Boundary Concepts in AT-LAB III

Conceptual Clarity (Civic register)
In AT-LAB III, conceptual clarity was tested not through academic framing but through collective public debate. Participants brought heterogeneous AI framings to the workshop (science-fiction, journalistic, experiential) and the workshop exposed this heterogeneity productively. Clarity was less an individual achievement than a temporarily shared framing — forged through the voting and discussion process.
Perceived Agency (Collective dimension)
Agency in AT-LAB III was irreducibly collective. Individual participants felt more confident questioning AI-generated imagery when doing so with others — suggesting that civic AI literacy is a community practice, not an individual skill. The live voting interface made collective preference visible in real time, creating a feedback loop between individual sense-making and group position.
Transferability (Toolkit potential)
AT-LAB III generated the most transferable outputs of any lab: the Promptare workshop format, the Semiotic Protocol card set, and the facilitation framework can all be applied in different civic contexts. The planned open toolkit represents the most explicit transferability claim of the trajectory.

Dissemination

  • Live intervention: Placemaking Week Europe 2025, Reggio Emilia
  • Planned output: Open toolkit and workshop protocol for civic AI literacy facilitation
  • Documentation of Mediatized Encounters activity
  • Link: oral presentation recorded at hanze.nl research portal

AT-LAB III closes the participatory arc of the trajectory — health, education, civic — and opens the question the whole trajectory has been building toward: can an artistic environment produce literacy not through explanation or discussion, but through direct perceptual experience? This is the question ATMOS_FERA answers.
