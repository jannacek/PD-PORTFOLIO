AT-LAB Methodology

The iterative design-research cycle at the core of the PD: five phases, four instantiations, one cumulative argument

The AT-LAB is the primary methodological contribution of this Professional Doctorate. It is not a label for four projects. It is a research form: a structured participatory environment that follows a consistent investigative cycle while adapting entirely to local conditions. What this makes possible is artistic research that is iterative, comparable across contexts, and epistemically rigorous—without losing its situated, practice-based character.

What an AT-LAB Is

An AT-LAB is an artistically designed research environment built around a situated question. Each one is a designed encounter—immersive installation, course, workshop, or performance—that creates conditions for AI and data literacy to emerge, be observed, and be reflected upon. The 'artistic' is not decorative. It is the epistemic engine: aesthetic design, sensory engagement, and relational encounter produce forms of understanding that instructional settings cannot.

The methodology treats artistic research as repeatable and comparable without universalising it. The same cycle runs in each lab; the content, medium, scale, and participants are entirely different. This is not a contradiction—it is the argument.

The feed-forward is not incidental. It is the mechanism by which the methodology accumulates knowledge across contexts. Each AT-LAB does not begin fresh—it begins from what the previous one made visible.

What Remains Constant

Across all four AT-LABs, the following remain structurally consistent:

  • An artistically designed participatory environment as the primary research site
  • A situated research question anchored in AI and data literacy
  • Lightweight qualitative instruments: questionnaires, curatorial reflection, dialogue-based analysis
  • Connection to a specific domain context and its stakeholders
  • A documented reflective feedback loop that generates feed-forward into the next iteration

Why This Form of Research Matters

AT-LABs demonstrate that artistic research can be structured as a repeatable, context-sensitive methodology without losing its situated character, artistic autonomy, or epistemic rigour. They prove that the artistic voice and the research voice are not in tension—they can be formally integrated.

The methodological claim is not that artistic research is equivalent to experimental science. It is that artistic research has its own form of iterability—one grounded in design, encounter, and reflection rather than controlled variables and statistical sampling. The AT-LAB is the structure that makes this claim operational.

The four iterations of this PD do not prove a universal claim about AI literacy. They demonstrate that a specific methodological form—artistically designed, participatory, iterative, reflective—can produce situated, transferable knowledge across radically different contexts. That is the contribution.
