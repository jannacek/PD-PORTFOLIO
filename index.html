AI & Data Literacy
through Artistic Research Environments

Four iterative AT-LABs — from a health community in Groningen to a planetary immersive performance in Amsterdam — operating as a single research engine for situated, transferable knowledge

AT-LAB Methodology Boundary Concepts EMI Framework

This Professional Doctorate investigates whether iterative participatory artistic environments can produce AI and data literacy — and what kind of knowledge that process generates. Four AT-LABs form one research engine across health, education, civic, and artistic contexts, each following a consistent cycle of design, intervention, micro-measure, reflection, and feed-forward. Three Boundary Concepts — Conceptual Clarity, Perceived Agency, Transferability — enable cross-context comparison. The EMI Framework positions the contribution within transdisciplinary collaboration. ATMOS_FERA — a live immersive audiovisual performance built from NASA planetary data — is the methodological capstone: the argument at full scale.

Formally registered as: Lifestyle and Digital Sovereignty: A new-media arts approach to collective digital empowerment for holistic care

Three Contribution Layers

The PD produces three distinct but interdependent contributions, each operating at a different level of abstraction above the individual AT-LABs.

Methodology
AT-LAB
Iterative artistic research environments as a repeatable, context-sensitive methodology. Each AT-LAB follows a consistent five-phase cycle — design, intervention, micro-measure, reflection, feed-forward — while adapting entirely to local conditions, participants, and artistic medium.
Comparative Layer
Boundary Concepts
Three diagnostic lenses — Conceptual Clarity, Perceived Agency, Transferability — extracted from practice and refined across all four AT-LABs. They enable cross-context comparison without collapsing local specificity, and form a minimal epistemic sequence: clarity → agency → transfer.
Framework Synthesis
EMI
Epistemic Mediation Indicators: a practitioner-facing qualitative framework for articulating how artistic research contributes to knowledge-making in transdisciplinary collaboration. Six dimensions for locating the epistemic function of artistic work — above the AT-LAB cycle, not within it.
