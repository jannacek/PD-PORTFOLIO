Four Roles

The four roles of the Professional Doctorate candidate as framework for integrated assessment

Important distinction: The four roles (Researcher, Innovator, Change Agent, Professional) form one assessment framework, articulated by Andriessen et al. (2024). The five learning goals form a separate assessment framework. These are complementary but distinct frameworks. Roles ≠ Learning goals.

The four roles of the PD Arts + Creative are integrated lenses through which the trajectory can be read. They are not separate competencies or boxes to be ticked. In this trajectory, they interpenetrate: the Researcher's inquiry cannot be separated from the Change Agent's civic intervention, or the Professional's teaching from the Innovator's methodological contribution. The following sections show how each role plays out across the full arc of the work.

Role I

Researcher

The Researcher role involves systematic inquiry, theoretical grounding, and the development of transferable knowledge. In this PD, the research dimension is present across all four AT-LABs through: triangulated qualitative methods (questionnaire, curatorial reflection, dialogue-based analysis in AT-LAB II); literature engagement across artistic research evaluation frameworks, transdisciplinary collaboration theory, and AI literacy studies; and the development of two transferable contributions—the Boundary Concepts framework and the EMI (Epistemic Mediation Indicators) vocabulary—that can be applied beyond this PD's specific contexts.

The Researcher does not only gather data. They develop new knowledge that is accessible to the profession and to the broader field. The P+ARTS 2025 peer-reviewed publication, the WG2 and WG4 contributions to the COST Action CA23158, and this portfolio are the primary vehicles for making that knowledge accessible. The research is grounded in explicit methodological choices: each AT-LAB has a research question, specific instruments (questionnaires, dialogue protocols, curatorial frameworks), and a feed-forward logic that ensures findings from one iteration inform the design of the next.

The trajectory demonstrates research maturity through: independent design of research instruments; capacity to work across disciplinary boundaries (health, teaching, civic engagement, artistic performance); rigorous documentation and reflection on method; and iterative refinement of research questions in response to evidence rather than predetermined outcomes.

Role II

Innovator

The Innovator develops approaches and solutions that have both specific and generic applicability. The primary innovation in this PD is formal: the AT-LAB methodology itself. It is specific in that each AT-LAB is entirely context-dependent (health data in t'Zandt, AI literacy in a master's course, placemaking in Reggio Emilia, atmospheric phenomena at ADE); it is generic in that the structural form—design, intervention, micro-measure, reflection, feed-forward—can be applied across domains and adapted to different research questions. This methodological portability is demonstrated across four consecutive implementations (AT-LAB I through IV), each building on and refining the previous model.

A second innovation is conceptual: the Boundary Concepts framework constitutes a new middle layer in the existing landscape of artistic research evaluation—between the universalising tendency of standard metrics and the purely local character of situated artistic judgment. This framework translates between different epistemic communities without flattening the differences between them. A third is the EMI (Epistemic Mediation Indicators) framework itself, as a practitioner-facing instrument for a specific and underserved need: artist-researchers working inside transdisciplinary collaborations who need language for their epistemic contribution.

ATMOS_FERA as AT-LAB IV also constitutes an innovation in form: a live performance as a fully articulated research environment, operating at scale (100+ audience members), with the audience as the participant group. This dissolves the boundary between artistic output and research, between performance and pedagogy, between aesthetic experience and data collection.

Role III

Change Agent

The Change Agent initiates and sustains processes of learning and change across individual, institutional, and field levels. In this PD, the change agent role is visible in multiple registers. At the community level: AT-LAB I engaged the t'Zandt health community with digital health data in a context where that engagement had previously been minimal, introducing artistic and sensory approaches to health literacy. At the institutional level: AT-LAB II redesigned a master's-level course at Frank Mohr Institute and introduced a new critical frame for AI engagement in arts education—one that has been adopted and continued in subsequent cohorts. The course evolution from 1 EC to 2 EC, and its integration across multiple programmes (MADTech, Painting, MAP, IRAP), demonstrates sustainable institutional change.

At the civic level: AT-LAB III contributed to Placemaking Week Europe's arts and culture stream, expanding how the network understands the role of artistic methods in civic AI literacy. The Promptare workshop and Semiotic Protocol have been designed for replicability across other city contexts. At the field level: contributions to COST Action CA23158 (WG2 and WG4) position the AT-LAB methodology and the EMI framework in an active European research community, influencing policy discourse around AI and artistic intelligence.

The change agent role also requires building genuine stakeholder ownership, not imposing change from outside. This has been a deliberate methodological commitment across all four AT-LABs: participatory design, informed consent, iterative co-creation with participants. In AT-LAB II, students were not research subjects but co-inquirers. In AT-LAB III, the Semiotic Protocol was a collective meaning-making tool, not a data-extraction mechanism.

Role IV

Professional

The Professional role involves domain expertise, ethical responsibility, and sustained self-reflection on practice and development. In this PD, the professional dimension includes: the active development of teaching practice across three iterations of the AI course at Frank Mohr Institute (2022–2025), with documented evolution from 1 EC to 2 EC and documented integration into multiple master's programmes; the maintenance and development of artistic practice throughout the research period (performances at Stone Nest London 2023, Sound & Vision 4bidGallery 2023, ATMOS_FERA 2025, residential training with 4DSOUND); engagement with professional networks (Sound Arguments at Orpheus Institute and ACPA Leiden, ARC in the North, AI Cluster at V2_ / ARIAS); and the ethical grounding of the research through data management planning, GDPR compliance, informed consent protocols, and ethics review for all AT-LABs.

Self-reflection is documented across the supervision process (three documented supervision sessions and feedback loops recorded in the Training and Supervision Plan), the Go-No-Go assessment (April 2023), and the iterative feed-forward logic of the AT-LABs themselves—each of which required critical reflection on the limitations of the previous iteration before the next could be designed. The transition from AT-LAB II to AT-LAB III involved explicit reflection on scale, participatory depth, and the role of artistic form. The transition to AT-LAB IV involved reflection on how to operationalise research at the scale of a full performance event.

Professional development also includes sustained engagement with artistic research methodologies through the Sound Arguments training programme (2025–2026), participation in research networks (COST Action, ARC in the North), and publication in peer-reviewed venues.

The four roles should not be read as separate boxes to tick. In this trajectory, they interpenetrate: the Researcher's inquiry cannot be separated from the Change Agent's civic intervention, or the Professional's teaching from the Innovator's methodological contribution. The four roles are four dimensions of a unified professional practice.
