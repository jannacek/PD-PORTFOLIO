PD Trajectory

Four AT-LABs as a single iterative arc: Health → Teaching → Civic → ATMOS_FERA

Four AT-LABs constitute one evolving investigation. Each iteration builds on the learning of the previous, tightening the methodology, refining the conceptual lenses, and expanding the contexts of inquiry. The trajectory moves from the intimate scale of bodily experience in a health community, through a teaching studio and a civic placemaking arena, to the planetary scale of an immersive artistic performance. What looks like four separate projects is, viewed correctly, one research engine iterating across contexts.

AT-LAB I · 2023 Health — Sensory Environments and Digital Sovereignty
Context
Healthy Living as a Service (HLaS), NWO Knowledge & Innovation Covenant, Work Package 2. Community of t'Zandt, Groningen. Partners: Amsterdam UMC, Hanze University, City of Groningen.
Intervention
Immersive installation with custom biosensors (arm sensors, heartbeat monitors), spatial sound, and choreographed light. 50+ community participants. Designed to make health data tangible and relational rather than clinical.
Research Focus
Can a participatory artistic environment strengthen digital health literacy in a community setting? What does it mean to engage critically with data when the encounter is sensory and aesthetic?
Key Learning
'Wellbeing' proved too broad as a research frame. The environment created genuine engagement but the connection to AI/data literacy needed explicit scaffolding. Emphasis shifted toward how participants name and situate their experience.
Dissemination: SAR Conference 2025 poster (Porto) · European Researchers' Night 2023, Forum Groningen · HLaS consortium presentations · Full documentation →
Tightened research question · Lighter measurement instruments · Stronger emphasis on conceptual clarity · Introduced perceived agency as explicit research focus

AT-LAB II · 2024 Teaching — AI Literacy as Artistic Practice
Context
Frank Mohr Institute, Hanze University. Course: 'AI: In, Within, and Around Your Practice' (2 ECTS). Ten students across MADTech, Painting, MAP, and IRAP programmes.
Intervention
Structured semester-long course combining conceptual disambiguation, hands-on experimentation, critical literacy, and a culminating exhibition. Triangulated methodology: questionnaire, curatorial reflection, dialogue-based analysis.
Research Focus
How do emerging artists define, engage with, and imagine AI? What shifts when artistic practice—rather than instruction—becomes the site of AI literacy?
Key Learning
Students revealed a consistent gap between frequent AI use and hesitant artistic integration. Curation emerged as epistemic method. Three pedagogical parameters crystallised: begin with conceptual clarification; embed critical literacy throughout; use curation as research.
Dissemination: P+ARTS 2025 peer-reviewed publication (Naples) · Exhibition 'Artistic Research Encounters' (Frank Mohr Institute, 2024) · Full documentation →
Three parameters → formal Boundary Concepts · Curation as epistemic method feeds EMI · Confirmed transferability across domains

AT-LAB III · 2025 Civic — Collective Meaning-Making and AI
Context
Placemaking Week Europe 2025, Reggio Emilia. International civic participation arena. Collaboration with Placemaking Europe and PublicTwin platform for interactive spatial communication.
Intervention
Two complementary probes: 'Promptare!' workshop (generative AI for collective urban imagination) and 'Semiotic Protocol' game (structured group negotiation of meaning through signs and collective signals).
Research Focus
Can artistic methods strengthen AI and data literacy in civic participation contexts? What changes when literacy is understood as a collective rather than individual achievement?
Key Learning
Literacy benefits emerged from collective reflection and negotiated meaning-making, not individual instruction. Digital literacy is a social practice. The Boundary Concepts proved they can operate across radically different institutional and cultural contexts.
Dissemination: Placemaking Week Europe workshop (Reggio Emilia, Sept. 2025) · PD Arts + Creative Symposium (Tilburg, 2025) · Full documentation →
Collective meaning-making as capstone design principle · Boundary Concepts validated across civic scale · Full confidence to operate at performance scale

AT-LAB IV · 2025 ATMOS_FERA — Atmospheric Enactment as Epistemic Practice
Context
Amsterdam Dance Event Arts & Culture programme (ADE 2025). ISO Amsterdam. The capstone AT-LAB—where methodology, Boundary Concepts, and EMI framework converge in a single, ambitious artistic event.
Intervention
Live audiovisual performance (45–55 min). Seven acts tracing planetary atmospheres—Venus, Mars, Neptune, Jupiter, terrestrial return—via NASA Planetary Spectrum Generator data transformed into spatial sound, moving light, fog, and hologauze projection. Collaboration with KIKUNO (custom spatial audio systems). Custom shaders translate atmospheric data into real-time visual environments.
Research Focus
Can an immersive artistic environment function as an epistemic instrument—where AI and data literacy emerges through perception-as-inquiry rather than explanation?
Key Learning
Literacy can be staged as embodied encounter. 'To perceive is already to inhabit.' The performance demonstrated that all three Boundary Concepts (conceptual clarity, perceived agency, transferability) operate simultaneously at full scale. The AT-LAB methodology holds under ambitious artistic conditions.
Dissemination: ADE Arts & Culture 2025 · ISEA 2026 submission (planned) · Full documentation →
